Beta

The Bhalo!Bangla Beta program is online Bengali tutoring. The goal of the program is to help students maintian a connection with their family, nation, religion, and heritage.

so there's 3 parts:

  1. Tutoring
    One to three hours of live-online Bengali tutoring at $10/hour
  2. Resources
    Online resources (think vocab lists, activities, games) based on lesson plans
  3. Regular Feedback
    One paragraph of feedback after each lesson and oppotunities to provide feedback after each unit

I want more info on:

Goals
The goals of the program are functional. Thus, lessons will focus almost exclusively on developing practical skills, like conversations and comprehension.

The Unit Method
Units help make a vast language more manageable. Each unit spans ~6 weeks and focuses on particular themes and skills. See Unit 1 below

Measuring Growth
The growth of a student will be measured through auto-graded online activities. At the end of a unit, the teacher will report more comprehensively on student progress toward unit objectives.
You'll see a link to setup billing in your account after you register for the Beta Program. You can always change your billing method or the number of hours of tutoring afterward.
During onboarding, you'll be matched with a tutor based on preferred class times. You can always change your avaliability online.

We will constantly ask you for feedback to find answers to the questions that drive us: How can the Bengali community be served best? What are the issues facing Bengalis and how can they be fixed?

Both your participation and feedback are incredibly valuable. They will determine the direction of the program and locate the areas that need improvement.

All the channels of feedback we have avaliable to us will be utilized to direct how the program will develop this year. Here is the plan to gain and use your feedback:

    • Begin market research interviews (~10 interviews)
    • Onboard first 5 interested students
    • Ask teachers about student engagement
  • March 2023

    • Final market research interviews (~20 more)
